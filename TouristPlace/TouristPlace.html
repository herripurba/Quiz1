TOURIST PLACE

Talking about North Sumatra, it is certain that you will think of Lake Toba, which has extraordinary natural beauty, and even its name is worldwide. However, North Sumatra also has one area that has extraordinary tourism potential, namely Dairi. Apart from being one of the best coffee producing cities which is very famous in North Sumatra, Dairi also has several tourist attractions that are very pleasing to the eye.
Here are some of the tourist attractions in Sidikalang.

Sicike-cike Lake

Lae Pendaroh Waterfall originates in this lake, besides that there are 2 (two) other rivers that also head up in this lake, namely the Lae Simblin River which is in the same Regency, Dairi. And from Pakpak Bharat district, namely the Mbilulu river. Sicike-cike Lake is also considered sacred and has a high historical value, the people around the lake believe that in the past the place where the lake is located is the origin of the 7 (seven) clans of the Pakpak Batak tribe. These clans include: Angkat, Bintang, Capah, Gajah Manik, Kudadiri, Sinamo and Ujung. In addition to offering the beauty of the lake, on Sicike-cike Lake you can find various types of flora and fauna from natural origin of Sumatra. Friends can visit the address of Lae Hole Village, Parbuluan District, Dairi Regency.

Silalahi Lake

The location of Tao Silalahi is close to Lake Toba, so this place is suitable for those of you who want to enjoy the world-wide view of Lake Toba. The beauty of this place is a beautiful stretch of beach, accompanied by a backdrop of stunning green hills. It makes anyone sedated, and makes it feel at home for a long time there. Here, you can swim, go around the lake by boat, or just relax. Even you can surf here.

Taman Wisata Iman

Taman Wisata Iman (TWI) is a religious tourism area located in the Sitinjo hills, Sitinjo District. At this location, you can see 5 buildings of worship that are close together to become a symbol of tolerance in Dairi which stands firmly in a 13 hectare area. In addition to the building of worship, still at the TWI location, you can also enjoy Sampendabah Waterfall which is located just below Golgota Hill. The waterfall about 30 meters high with several levels comes from the Lae (River) Pandaroh which flows and divides the TWI area. You don't need to hesitate about its safety because to the location of the waterfall there is already a footbridge complete with stairs and an iron safety fence. You can take advantage of several photo spots for selfies with a direct background of a waterfall surrounded by beautiful nature and fresh air. Several rides are also offered at Firdaus Park, which is still at the TWI location, such as a three-dimensional painting room, outbound facilities, a glass hallway and a laser room in the snake building as well as lodging on Noah's Ark and lodging at Muslim locations. Culinary places such as cafes and several places where souvenir sellers are lined up in locations that you are ready to take home as souvenirs for your family and friends.

Lau Timah baths

Unlike other baths in general, which have warm or even hot water, the Lau Timah baths are cold. The water comes from crevices in the rock, which is uniquely bright blue. In addition to presenting a beautiful view of the surroundings, the water in this bath turns out to have properties. It is said that the water in this bath can cure various diseases, such as gout, or skin diseases. Many people have proven the efficacy of this water.

Siadtaratas Hill

This hill overlooks Tao Toba so the scenery is super duper really beautiful. Every weekend this hill is always packed with visitors. Moreover, young people, they used to set up tents on this hill. So, if you want to go to this iconic place, you should come with friends. Siadtaratas Silalahi Hill is one of the most beautiful spots for travelers to try. Especially for those who like nature and adventure tourism. Siadtaratas Hill is filled with vast grasslands and routes that are not difficult to reach, making Siadtaratas Hill a very good place to enjoy the view of Lake Toba and wait for the sunset in the afternoon.

Lae Renun River

This river is the longest river in Dairi. With its swift currents, local residents use it for rafting. So, for tourists who want to try this activity, they should also use a guide so that their safety is guaranteed. Later, along the way along the river, you will witness the natural beauty that is very windah.

Lae Mbilulu Waterfall

The attraction of this waterfall is that there are two waterfalls that flow simultaneously, so it is also called twin waterfalls. To be able to enjoy this waterfall, you have to try a little first, because the journey to this waterfall is quite difficult. Before reaching this waterfall, you have to go through hills, suspension bridges, and also slippery roads. But all will pay off when you reach the waterfall. The waterfall here is clear.
